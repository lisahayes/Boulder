Boulder charges for water in five blocks based on your personal monthly water budget. Frequently Asked Questions

How do I read my Boulder water meter?

The City of Boulder meter sits in a small box at the curb. Lift the lid and read the digital display or dial face. It shows cumulative gallons or cubic feet. Note the reading, wait at least 2 hours with all fixtures off, then read again. Any increase confirms an active pressurized leak.

What is the City of Boulder water budget rate system?

Boulder assigns each customer a personalized monthly water budget based on lot size, number of bedrooms, and past use. Usage within the budget is billed at Block 1 ($5.65/1,000 gal). Usage above the budget climbs to Block 3 ($15.06), Block 4 ($22.58), or Block 5 ($37.64) per 1,000 gallons. A hidden leak that runs 24 hours a day can push a household well above budget within a single billing cycle.

Does a winter leak raise my Boulder sewer bill?

Yes. Boulder uses an Average Winter Consumption (AWC) figure based on December through March bills to set your wastewater cap for the rest of the year. A hidden leak running during those months inflates the AWC and raises your wastewater charges for the following 12 months, on top of the immediate water bill impact.

Does a slab leak cause a high water bill in Boulder?

Almost always. A slab leak is a pressurized supply pipe failure under the concrete foundation that runs continuously. Even a moderate slab leak at 5 to 10 gallons per hour adds 3,600 to 7,200 gallons per month. At Boulder rates, that can push usage into Block 3 or Block 4, significantly increasing the monthly bill before any visible damage appears.
