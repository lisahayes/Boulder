Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a dripping faucet cost per year in Boulder CO?

At a steady 60 drips per minute, a faucet wastes about 5.5 gallons per day, or roughly 2,000 gallons per year. At Block 1 water rates ($5.65/1,000 gal) plus Boulder wastewater ($8.86/1,000 gal), that is about $29 per year. A household already near its water budget may see the excess billed at Block 3 ($15.06/1,000 gal), raising the annual cost significantly.

What are the City of Boulder water budget blocks?

Boulder assigns each residential customer a personalized monthly water budget. Block 1 ($5.65/1,000 gal) covers usage up to 60 percent of the budget. Block 2 ($7.53) covers 61 to 100 percent. Block 3 ($15.06) covers 101 to 150 percent. Block 4 ($22.58) covers 151 to 200 percent. Block 5 ($37.64) covers use above 200 percent of the budget. These are 2026 rates for a standard 3/4-inch meter.

Does a toilet leak push me into a higher water budget block in Boulder?

A running toilet wastes about 480 gallons per day, or about 14,600 gallons per month. Adding that to a typical household's 4,000 to 6,000 gallons per month would push well above the water budget into Block 3 or higher at $15.06 to $37.64 per 1,000 gallons. Call (303) 552-3896 to get the leak found and fixed before the next bill cycle.

Does a winter leak raise my Boulder wastewater bill?

Yes. Boulder sets your wastewater cap using the Average Winter Consumption from December through March. A leak during those months inflates the AWC and raises your wastewater cap for the following 12 months.
